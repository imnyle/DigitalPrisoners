ABOUT THE PROJECT
On the internet, identity is freely constructed and reconstructed into new visions of self. In its malleability, the black box of algorithms becomes the black box of a theatre as users must perform the expectations of online identity as they individualize themselves.
Become the Binary: Digital Prisoners is inspired by this experience as it pertains to masculinity. Young men have begun entering cyberspaces upheld by the performance of toxic masculinity, and a rejection of anything else. The result is a culture of young men trying to find themselves through cycles of hatred and bigotry, and coming out worse for it on the other end.

This piece has audience members “perform masculinity” by performing 4 machismo poses in front of a webcam. An algorithm then decides if your poses are manly enough, and punishes you if they are not. As you switch between the prescribed pose options, the piece reflects back a sculpture abstracted from one of the poses. The internet's algorithms and harmful subcultures distort ideas of masculinity until they are unrecognizable and full of hatred, reflecting these abstractions back onto their community members. This piece visualizes that.

The abstractions are inspired by Michelangelo's unfinished “prisoners”. Michelangelo was famous for his depictions of the male form, and in these unfinished pieces, we see that form yearning to release itself from a prison of marble stone. My work brings this concept to the digital realm, with my figure entrapped in the sculptural glitches extracted from a photogrammetry algorithm.

The poem included is about Michelangelo's prisoners, and in this case, about male cyberculture.
